**1. Example 1 – Rate of change of the area of a circle with respect to its radius**  
1. Write the formula for the area of a circle: \(A=\pi r^{2}\).  
2. Differentiate \(A\) with respect to \(r\) to obtain the rate of change: \(\dfrac{dA}{dr}=2\pi r\).  
3. Substitute the given radius \(r=5\) cm: \(\dfrac{dA}{dr}=2\pi(5)=10\pi\) cm² / s.  
Thus, when the radius is 5 cm, the area is increasing at a rate of \(10\pi\) cm² / s. [Rate of Change of Quantities] concept=concept.872cbd522bc5 field=example_texts locator=0  

**2. Example 2 – How fast the surface area of a cube is increasing**  
1. Let \(x\) be the edge length of the cube; then volume \(V=x^{3}\) and surface area \(S=6x^{2}\).  
2. The given rate of change of volume is \(\dfrac{dV}{dt}=9\) cm³ / s.  
3. Differentiate \(V=x^{3}\) with respect to time: \(\dfrac{dV}{dt}=3x^{2}\dfrac{dx}{dt}\).  
4. Solve for \(\dfrac{dx}{dt}\): \(\dfrac{dx}{dt}= \dfrac{9}{3x^{2}}=\dfrac{3}{x^{2}}\). (1)  
5. Differentiate the surface‑area formula: \(\dfrac{dS}{dt}=12x\dfrac{dx}{dt}\).  
6. Substitute \(\dfrac{dx}{dt}\) from (1): \(\dfrac{dS}{dt}=12x\left(\dfrac{3}{x^{2}}\right)=\dfrac{36}{x}\).  
7. With \(x=10\) cm, \(\dfrac{dS}{dt}= \dfrac{36}{10}=3.6\) cm / s.  
Hence, when the edge length is 10 cm, the surface area is increasing at \(3.6\) cm / s. [Rate of Change of Quantities] concept=concept.872cbd522bc5 field=example_texts locator=1  

**3. Example 3 – Rate at which the area of a circular wave expands**  
1. The area of a circle is \(A=\pi r^{2}\).  
2. Differentiate with respect to time using the chain rule: \(\dfrac{dA}{dt}=2\pi r\,\dfrac{dr}{dt}\).  
3. The wave speed gives \(\dfrac{dr}{dt}=4\) cm / s.  
4. Insert \(r=10\) cm: \(\dfrac{dA}{dt}=2\pi(10)(4)=80\pi\) cm² / s.

**4. Example 4 – Changes in the perimeter and area of a rectangle**  
1. The length \(x\) is decreasing at \(\dfrac{dx}{dt}=-3\) cm / min, and the width \(y\) is increasing at \(\dfrac{dy}{dt}=2\) cm / min.  
2. **Perimeter:** \(P=2(x+y)\). Differentiate: \(\dfrac{dP}{dt}=2\left(\dfrac{dx}{dt}+\dfrac{dy}{dt}\right)=2(-3+2)=-2\) cm / min.  
3. **Area:** \(A=xy\). Differentiate: \(\dfrac{dA}{dt}=x\dfrac{dy}{dt}+y\dfrac{dx}{dt}\).  
4. Substitute \(x=10\) cm, \(y=6\) cm, \(\dfrac{dx}{dt}=-3\), \(\dfrac{dy}{dt}=2\):  
   \(\dfrac{dA}{dt}=10(2)+6(-3)=20-18=2\) cm² / min.
